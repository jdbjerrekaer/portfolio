## Overview

LeadPlatform is Adtraction's lead-generation system — the product for campaigns where the conversion is a *lead* (someone submitting their name, phone or email) rather than an e-commerce sale. Instead of a plain newsletter form, brands run interactive, gamified microsites that entertain people while collecting qualified leads, distributed through the Nordic affiliate network. The system began at Adservice and is now a standalone Angular service that I design and build.

## What I built

This is a design-engineering project, not a design hand-off — I work across the Angular front-end and the tooling around it:

- **Angular platform** — the campaign microsites are built in Angular 19 (TypeScript, pnpm), with a shared component and theming layer so every new campaign inherits a consistent, on-brand system.
- **Shared form & UI component library** — a reusable set of form primitives (inputs, selects, checkboxes, date and birthdate pickers, buttons) with a live preview page, so every campaign's forms behave and look the same without being rebuilt each time.
- **`create-leadplatform` scaffolding CLI** — a generator that spins up a new branded campaign (quiz, wheel, contest, scratcher) from a template, themes it to the client's brand, wires up routing and state, and configures the campaign settings in a single command.
- **Automated banner pipeline** — a Satori → resvg → sharp toolchain that renders ad banners, prize tiles and social/OG share images to WebP from templates, so campaign creatives don't have to be cut by hand.
- **Hub previews** — auto-captured screenshot tiles for the platform hub, kept in sync as new campaigns are added.

## The lead flow

Behind the interactive front-end is a real lead pipeline:

- a user completes a quiz, wheel or contest on an affiliate's landing page and submits their details;
- the submission is validated and de-duplicated — required fields present, no repeat entries, the affiliate authorised for the campaign;
- qualified leads are forwarded to each advertiser's own integration;
- the lead receives a GDPR consent and confirmation email, with a self-service flow to withdraw consent at any time.

The service is containerised and deployed to AWS behind a CDN and WAF.

## Campaign formats

LeadPlatform ships several gamified formats, each a value exchange that makes people *want* to share their details:

### Quiz

Interactive questionnaires that capture curiosity along with contact details, instead of asking people to fill in a static form.

### Prize Wheel

Spin-to-win mechanics with a clear, immediate reward for entering.

### Contest

Raffle signups where the prize justifies sharing information — a clean value exchange that motivates people to opt in.

### Scratcher

Instant-win scratch cards for fast, low-friction entry.

### Advent Calendar

Seasonal day-by-day calendars that bring people back to open a new door — and a new chance to enter — across a whole campaign period.

## Impact

The gamified approach delivers measurable results:

- **60+ live branded campaigns** shipped across the Nordic market — for brands including UNICEF, Telia, DNA, Talkmore, Fortum and Plan BørneFonden — from a single templated platform
- **207,992 clicks** in a 20-day period — a **384.92% increase** versus the preceding period (from 42,892 clicks)
- **7,553 conversions** captured — a **43.51% increase** versus the preceding period (from 5,263 conversions)
- **+31% average conversion rate** for quiz-based lead capture
- **Full-service** — design, traffic driving and integration handled by one partner
- **Distribution** through the Nordic affiliate network
- **Pay-per-site pricing**, with no yearly licensing

## Why it works

LeadPlatform turns lead generation from a passive form-filling exercise into something people actually want to play. Just as important, the engineering around it — a scaffolding CLI, a templated component system, and an automated creative pipeline — means a new branded campaign can go from brief to live in a fraction of the usual time, without sacrificing brand consistency or quality.
